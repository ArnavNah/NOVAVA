Origin

Breaking the flat screen paradigm.

For a decade, web design accepted the limitations of flat pixels. We aligned content to static planes and rigid boxes. But natural human focus expects depth, parallax, and material reactions.

Vortex was designed to bridge this gap. What if web interfaces behaved like organic glass membranes? What if a panel could refract ambient canvas light and warp under cursor momentum?

By mapping CSS layouts directly into custom vertex shaders, we merge standard DOM utility with high-end experiential graphics. It is not decorative; it is tactile.

Mechanism

Designed for infinite layers.

Transcend traditional z-index stacks. The Vortex pipeline projects normal HTML coordinates directly into multi-dimensional WebGL layers.

01. Quantum Materials

Define tactile material attributes directly in your layouts. Our compiler parses values like roughness, opacity, and refraction into highly optimized shader passes.

02. Ray-Traced Physics

Simulate light paths directly across the DOM. Volumetric shadows, highlights, and caustic refractions respond dynamically to cursor motion and scroll speed.

03. Volumetric Pipeline

Pipe complex visual effects like blur-depths, chromatic aberration, and noise filters directly into your component lifecycle using a simple declarative system.
